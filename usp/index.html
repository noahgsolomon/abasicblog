Consumer Intent

Consumers do not want to search and look into your entire website for 10 minutes before purchasing an item. They want the experience to be simple and unique.

your Unique Selling Proposition (USP) should be short and concise and describe what purpose your product serves.

your Call To Action (CTA) and USP should be clear in the start screen of your website.

The goal should be to give your customers certainty that theyre in the right place, give your customers a quick pitch. Your CTA needs to be concise so that your customers do not get bored and leave your page.
1.) Save your customers time.

2.) Make them feel theyre saving money on your product or that they will make money with your product.

3.) Give the customer certainty that your product will benefit them

Finding Your USP

In order to find your Unique Selling Proposition, you must understand who your target audience is. This will help you understand who your competitors are as well as how to cater to your target audience

Develop a unique process so you can repeat it.

A --------------> B
A = consumer
----> = identifying problem and what experience the customer would get 'working with you'
B = purchase

your USP needs to be just that... UNIQUE. yes it is a good idea to learn from your competition but your goal should be to bring something new to the table. the user experience and interface needs to be AMAZING to give your brand more credibility and your customer more certainty.

Get in the customers shoes. Focus on the buying experience. Make it ENJOYABLE not like a process.

Do not seek to solve a problem for everyone, just focus on your target audience and what problem they need to have solved
(be clear in identifying problem).

Selling Someones Product Vs Creating a Product

There are two types of products you can sell. you can either sell someone elses product or create your own product.

Selling Someones Product

When selling someone elses product, you can either dropship it or wholesale.

    Dropshipping:
  • Pay per order. 0 inventory

  • good to test how products move (less risk)

  • Dont have much room for customizability in product
    Wholesale:
  • Higher risk than dropshipping because money is needed upfront (not pay per order).

  • Buying in bulk quantity.

Creating a Product

When creating your own product, you can either make your product on your own, make it through a manufacturer, or Print on Demand (for clothing)

    Make:
  • Hard to Scale.
    Manufacturer:
  • Most scalable and highest upside potential.

  • Largest upfront risk of your time and money.

  • Can take months to develop a product this way.
    Print on Demand
  • 0 inventory way of making clothing with print on it.

Finding a Supplier

  • Find manufacturer
    (use oberlo if dropshipping)

  • contact them, send them designs, ideas, samples, and negotiate bulk minimum and pricing.

  • Receieve prototypes.

  • Send back adjustments.

  • Receive final product.

What Makes a Winning Product?

In order to see what products are winners and which are losers ask these questions...

  • What is the potential market size?

  • What does the competitive landscaper look like?

  • Is the market growing, declining, flat, or a fad?

  • Is your product locally available? If it is, then there is less incentive for the consumer to buy online.

  • Who is your target audience and what are their problems that they need solved?

Product Based Criteria

You need to ask yourself these questions about your product in order to understand what you need to determine the profitibility and to ensure the customers certainty.


  • What is your markup? (%)

  • What is your potential selling price?

  • Can you offer a subscription with your product?

  • What is product size & weight?

  • How durable is your product? (If it is fragile, you might want to offer a warranty and free return)

  • Is your product seasonal? Will you experience higher traffic at a certain point in the year?

  • What problem does you product solve?/What is the purpose of your product?

  • Scalability of the market of the product.

How Do I Evaluate My Competition?

To clarify, competition is not a bad thing. it shows that there is demand in your market. To find your competitors you can search long tail and short tail keywords about your market.
You can use applications like SemRush to see your competitions traffic volume and the amount of backlinks they have and where their traffic is coming from. It is important to see their UX/UI, and their USP and how well they display their CTA.
